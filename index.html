What is Pseudomonas aeruginosa?

Pseudomonas aeruginosa is a deadly bacteria commonly found in the environment or near water.

It is one of the primary example of antibiotic resistance, with resistance rates up to 22.4% against Imipenem. It can cause a wide array of infections, including, but not limited to, blood, ear, eye, lung, and skin. With over 32,000 infections in 2017 alone, this bacteria should be diligently avoided. Proper hygiene techniques, such as hand-washing and not touching your face can go a long way in preventing an infection.
